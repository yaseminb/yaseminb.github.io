About

I completed my Ph.D. at the Royal Institute of Technology (KTH), in Stockholm, Sweden, in 2012. My PhD work was a part of the EU project CogX. Then, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher at KTH, involved in EU projects RoboHow and eSMCs, and at University of Birmingham, UK for the EU project RoMaNs and as a research scientist at ABB, Corporate Research, Sweden, coordinating the EU project SARAFun. I am currently a roboticist at Vicarious AI, CA where I lead a team of researchers working on grasping and manipulation planning. My research interests include robotics, computer vision and machine learning. More specifically, I work on learning-based approaches for robot grasping and manipulation using multisensory data.
